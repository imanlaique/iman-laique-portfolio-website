Product

Aloe is a Language Learning app designed for individuals seeking specialized language proficiency. The app features immersive resources, personalized learning, and live sessions with native speakers.

Background

Mastering a new language can be intimidating. Many available language learning apps lack a comprehensive strategy for fostering retention, overlooking essential elements such as cultural contexts, expert feedback, and diverse, non-repetitive activities.

PRODUCT FEATURES

Connecting with Speakers Feature

The connect feature allows users to filter through native speakers from different regions that match their preferences.

Review Feature

The review feature allows users to relearn activities they may have missed while progressing through their learning modules.

Flashcards Feature

The flashcards feature allows users to save words they’d like to go over again from their review activities.

DISCOVER

Competitors

To better understand current market trends, I conducted competitive analyses for three popular language learning apps — Babbel, Duolingo, and Rosetta Stone.

Insights:

  • Babbel was the easiest to use. The navigation was straightforward and it had resources for a mixed mode of learning preference. The interface was aesthetic, and I felt inspired to learn about the Spanish language and culture.
  • Duolingo is a great option for language learners, but it overemphasized a rewards system, which was distracting from the main goal. Nevertheless, the app was fun and simple to navigate.
  • Rosetta Stone was the least engaging app to use and it employed very traditional modes of learning and was designed for an older demographic.

User Research

I spoke to three individuals with prior experience with language-learning apps. Below are a couple passages offering their respective perspectives.

“I like to go to Mexico once a year. I'm really interested in the language. I want to be able to communicate with the people there and I just want to understand the culture a little bit more so I was looking into an app for that”.

“Sometimes the minigames you play didn't help me that much. It just felt repetitive, which is sometimes the point but in some cases, it felt like it was wasting my time, and made me feel burnt out”.

“[With other apps], it would have been cool if I was speaking to someone or if there were prompts to come up with answers or utilize the vocab rather than typing a sentence over and over. I'd rather learn with someone who speaks that language and have more interactive activity”.

Research Insights:

  • Each participant felt that fun activities were engaging and helpful, but concerned that repetitive activities would not aid in their language learning.
  • I had presumed that the main dissatisfaction with language apps would revolve around retention. Yet, as the participants shared, a common thread wove – a desire for immersive learning and communication with native speakers.
  • Participants expressed interest in context-specific activities such as those that might adhere to career-specific learning.
  • Review is important as it solidifies language knowledge over time and ensures language comes more naturally to users.

DEFINE

Persona

Insights from user research revealed that many language activities are generic and lack real-world application. Thus, I wanted to integrate career-specific activities and perspectives to empower individuals in their daily lives. The AAMC found that ~40 million Spanish speakers reside in the U.S., yet the number of physicians who speak Spanish is below 6%. The intersection of healthcare trends and user needs gave rise to the persona, Aria Klinik.

Usability Testing

I began usability testing with my mid-fidelity prototype. Though the general response from users was positive, below are a few features users believed needed to be refined and iterations.

Iterations

Onboarding- add example categories to select from

Review section- sort by lesson instead + add description

Improve home screen- add widget, cancel option, specify curriculum

Refine results- provide multiple options to continue

Filter speakers- by gender, age, profession, etc.

Expand on appointment confirmation- make personal, add app info

Word Bank- add description + make colorful

Decks- rearrange + make new deck stand out

Future Directions

Although this isn’t in the scope of the sprint, these are a few changes I’d still like to make on the platform.

Making Career-specific curriculum: Huge benefit of application that needs to be developed so people can complete activities based on their profession and learn the vocabulary they will need in real life

Expanding on Browse Section: Due to time constraints, I have not yet developed a browse section that gives users a taste of the culture that influenced the language they are learning.

Improving UI: I’d like to add more detail to my prototype. Some examples of features I’d like to include are a scroll function, adding photos, and actualizing the filter function, etc.

REFLECTION

In just a few weeks I was able to learn so much designing this app. When it comes to language learning, there is still a lack of global perspective in authentically learning a new language. Through future iterations, I hope to tackle some of these shortcomings. Even though, certain features like career-specific practice didn't come to fruition, the time constraints of this project taught me the value of prioritizing certain features.
